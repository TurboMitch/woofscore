Can dogs eat onion?

🧅 Toxic — never feed

Onions (raw, cooked or powdered) damage red blood cells and can cause anaemia. All forms, including onion powder in seasonings, are toxic.

If eaten: Watch for weakness, pale gums or dark urine and call your vet.

Why onion is dangerous

Onions (raw, cooked or powdered) damage red blood cells and can cause anaemia. All forms, including onion powder in seasonings, are toxic.

Symptoms to watch for

Signs can include vomiting, diarrhoea, lethargy, tremors, a racing heart, or weakness. Symptoms may be delayed, so don't wait for them to appear.

What to do if your dog ate onion

Watch for weakness, pale gums or dark urine and call your vet. Have the amount, your dog's weight and the time of ingestion ready. You can also call the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center. Do not induce vomiting unless a professional tells you to.
